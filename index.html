  My CS Journey  





My passion for computer science started the summer before sophomore year. I had stumbled upon CS professor David Malan's lectures on YouTube and became fascinated with the subject. It was then I completed my first coding project using Scratch, a block-based programming software. I created a game centered around a teenage girl, Ruby, who starts her first day at a new school. The code is very basic, but not bad for my first time!





Fast forward one year later, I joined Girls Who Code, an organization that aims to support and increase the number of women in computer science. In July, I participated in the Summer Immersion Program, which culminated in me coding a final project using JavaScript. This collection-style game was much more advanced than my previous one, and focused on restoring life to a post-apocalyptic world. Players needed to collect seeds and water buckets without clicking on zombies.





Most recently, I submitted a website I developed to the 2025 National STEM Festival. Using HTML and CSS, as well as research I did regarding adolescent attitudes, I created a digital information hub called "NY, Let's Talk About Climate Change" to raise awareness about the escalating threat of global warming. The platform consolidates important resources and climate-related discoveries in New York and across the U.S., making it easier for adolescents to inform themselves.

  CS Honors  




AiC Award Winner

The NCWIT Aspirations in Computing (AiC) Award recognizes young women for their computing-related achievements and leadership. As the Hudson Valley Affiliate Winner, I was honored for my technical skills and passion for computer science. This award has further fueled my desire to pursue a career in web-development and create a more inclusive future in the tech community.
